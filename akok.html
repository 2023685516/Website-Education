Kuih Akok

A nostalgic East Coast dessert — sweet, creamy, and slightly chewy. Traditionally baked with charcoal to achieve its signature wrinkled texture and deep eggy aroma.

50 Mins
Easy
20 Pieces

Bahan-bahan

5 biji telur ayam (saiz A)
200 ml santan pekat
100–150 g gula Melaka (diracik halus)
30–50 g tepung gandum
100 ml air
3–4 helai daun pandan (disimpul)
Sedikit garam
Minyak masak (untuk sapuan acuan)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Make the Palm Sugar Syrup: In a small pot, combine the chopped gula Melaka, water, and pandan leaves. Heat until the sugar dissolves completely. Let it cool down completely before mixing with eggs.
2
Prepare the Batter: In a blender or large bowl, combine the cooled syrup, coconut milk, eggs, wheat flour, and a pinch of salt. Blend or whisk until smooth and slightly frothy. Strain through a sieve to remove any lumps for a silky texture.
3
Preheat & Grease: Preheat your oven to 180°C. Lightly grease the akok molds or a cupcake tray with oil. Heat the empty greased tray in the oven for about 2 minutes before pouring the batter to ensure the kuih doesn't stick.
4
Bake the Kuih: Pour the batter into the hot molds until about ¾ full. Bake for 15–20 minutes. The kuih will puff up significantly in the oven and turn golden brown with some dark "charred" spots.
5
Cool & Serve: Once removed from the oven, the kuih will deflate and create its signature beautiful wrinkles. Let them cool slightly before removing from the molds. Best enjoyed warm with a cup of tea.
