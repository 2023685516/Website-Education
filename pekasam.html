Ikan Pekasam

A heritage dish from Perak where freshwater fish are preserved through fermentation with salt and toasted rice. The result is a savory, sour, and deeply satisfying appetite whetter when fried.

15 Mins (Cook)
Main Dish
Traditional

For Fermentation

Freshwater Fish (Lampam/Puyu)
Coarse Salt
2 cups Raw Rice
Tamarind Pieces (Asam Keping)

For Frying

Prepared Pekasam Fish
Big Onions (sliced)
Bird's Eye Chilies (Cili Padi)
Cooking Oil

Watch Traditional Method

Maktok's guide to authentic Northern Ikan Pekasam

Preparation Steps

1
Salting: Clean the fish thoroughly. Rub with plenty of salt and let it sit overnight to draw out moisture and firm up the flesh. [00:01:49]
2
Toasting the Rice: Fry raw rice in a dry pan (without oil) until it turns a deep golden brown. Once cooled, blend or grind it into a coarse powder. [00:00:53]
3
The Coating: Take the salted fish and coat it generously with the toasted rice powder, ensuring some goes into the belly cavity. Store in an airtight container for 2-3 weeks at room temperature to ferment. [00:02:27]
4
Deep Frying: To serve, heat oil and fry the fish with its rice coating until crispy. Add sliced onions and chilies towards the end until they are caramelized. Serve with hot steamed rice.
