Laksa Sarawak

Spicy and savory Sarawak laksa with noodles seafood and a fragrant coconut-based soup.

45 Minutes
Medium
4-5 Servings

Ingredients

Rice vermicelli (bihun), blanched
Sarawak Laksa paste(500 g)
500 g prawns, flesh separated
Chicken breast
1 liter water
Lemongrass & ginger (Optional)
500 ml coconut milk
Salt and a little sugar

Sambal (blended & sautéed):

Shallots, garlic, dried chilies
Driep shrimp & toasted shrimp paste
Tamarind water
Salt, sugar, anchovy cube

Toppings & Garnishes:

Fried tofu & tofu puffs
Fish balls or fish cake
Omelette, thinly slicedr
Coriander leaves
Calamansi limes

Preparation Steps

1
Sauté the blended ingredients until fragrant and oily. Season with salt, sugar, and tamarind water. Cook until thick and glossy.
2
Lightly fry the prawn flesh, then set aside. Fry prawn shells and heads, blend with cold water, and set aside.
3
Boil water with lemongrass and ginger. Add chicken and stock cubes; cook until ¾ done. Remove and set aside.
4
Strain the prawn stock into the chicken broth.
5
Add Sarawak laksa paste and bring to a boil. Stir in coconut milk and adjust seasoning.
6
Season chicken with a little turmeric and salt, lightly fry, then shred.
7
Season and lightly fry tofu and tofu puffs, then slice.
8
Blanch fish cake, fish balls, bean sprouts (with a little oil), and noodles. Slice coriander and cut calamansi.
9
Make a thin omelette, roll, and slice finely.
10
Add noodles to a bowl, pour over the broth, and serve with all toppings and sambal.
