Nasi Ganja

A famous version of Nasi Kandar from Ipoh, nicknamed "Ganja" because of its addictive spicy red sambal (sambal nyior) and rich mixture of various curries poured over fragrant yellow rice.

90 Mins
Heavy
Traditional

Yellow Rice (Nasi)

4 cups Basmati Rice (soaked)
Ghee / Margarine
Spices: Cinnamon, Star Anise, Cloves
Turmeric Powder (for color)
Evaporated Milk

The Components

Ayam Merah (Red Chicken)
Salted Egg (Telur Masin)
Okra (Bendi)
Cucumber slices

The "Ganja" Sambal

Grated Coconut (Kerisik style)
Dried Chili Paste
Shallots & Mint Leaves

How to make the addictive Nasi Ganja at home

Preparation Steps

1
The Fragrant Rice: Saut√© shallots, garlic, ginger, and dry spices (cinnamon, star anise, cloves) in ghee. Add pandan leaves and curry leaves for aroma. Stir in the turmeric powder and evaporated milk before adding water and the soaked basmati rice. [00:03:26]
2
The Sambal (Chutney): This is the "ganja" part! Prepare a coconut-based sambal (Sambal Nyior) by pounding together grated coconut, chilies, shallots, and a hint of lime. It should be spicy and slightly tangy.
3
The Curries (Kuah Campur): To get the authentic taste, you need a mixture of "Banjir" (flooded) gravies. Combine chicken curry, fish curry, and dalcha. The secret is the "Kuah Hitam" (dark gravy) mixed with the red spicy chicken gravy.
4
Plating: Serve a mound of the yellow rice. Add a piece of fried chicken or Ayam Merah. Place half a salted egg and some okra on the side. Finally, "flood" the rice with the mixed gravies and top it with a dollop of the signature sambal. [00:09:52]
