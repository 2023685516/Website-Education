Nasi Tumpang

An iconic Kelantanese breakfast "to-go". Soft, layered rice packed inside a banana leaf cone, filled with aromatic fish gulai, serunding, and fluffy omelette.

50 Mins
Medium
6 Packet

Ingredients

Nasi:

500g beras (dimasak lembik)
1000ml air
Daun pisang secukupnya

Gulai Ikan:

200g ikan kembung/tongkol
500ml santan pekat
Kunyit, lengkuas, bawang merah & cili kering (dikisar)

Inti Telur & Tambahan:

5 biji telur (dibuat dadar)
Bawang besar & daun bawang
Serunding ikan/daging

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Cook the Soft Rice: Rinse the rice and cook it with a higher water ratio (1:2) until it reaches a very soft, mashed-like consistency (similar to nasi himpit). Allow it to cool slightly so it's easier to handle.
2
Prepare the Gulai Ikan: Simmer the ground spices with coconut milk in a pot. Once fragrant, add the fish and cook until the gravy thickens into a rich, concentrated curry. Season with salt and sugar.
3
Make the Omelette: Whisk eggs with onions and chilies. Fry the omelette until golden and set. Slice it into small pieces or strips that can be easily layered.
4
Layering (The Tumpang Part): Create a cone shape using a softened banana leaf. Fill the bottom with a layer of rice and press down. Add a layer of serunding, followed by more rice, then omelette, and finally the gulai.
5
Seal and Serve: Finish with a final layer of rice to seal the fillings. Fold the top of the banana leaf and secure it with a toothpick. Serve the parcels as a complete, portable meal.
