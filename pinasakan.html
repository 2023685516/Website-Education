Pinasakan

A traditional Kadazandusun preserved fish dish from Sabah made by cooking fish with sour fruits to keep it fresh for long journeys.

40 Minutes
Easy
4 Servings

Ingredients

5–6 mackerel fish
4 cloves garlic, sliced
½ large onion, sliced
1 piece fresh turmeric, sliced
5 dried chilies, cut into pieces
2 large red chilies, sliced
2 tomatoes, quartered
3 tsp RASA SIFU seasoning
½ tsp sugar
2 tbsp tamarind juice
3 pieces asam gelugur
5 cups water

Preparation Steps

1
Boiling the Base: In a pot, combine water, garlic, onion, dried chilies, asam gelugur, and tamarind juice. Cover and bring to a boil.
2
Cooking the Fish: Add the fish, RASA SIFU (or salt to taste), sugar, tomatoes, and red chilies. Cover and simmer until the fish is fully cooked.
3
Serving: Serve hot with steamed rice for a flavorful meal.
4
NOTES: Traditionally, the dish includes bambangan or belimbing buluh; tomatoes can be used as a substitute. RASA SIFU can be replaced with salt if preferred.
