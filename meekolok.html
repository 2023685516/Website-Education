Mee Kolok

Tender noodles from Sarawak served with flavorful soy sauce fresh toppings and a hint of aromatics.

60 Minutes
Hard
5-6 Servings

Ingredients

Soup:

1 soup spice sachet
250 g beef, thinly sliced
½ beef stock cube
Salt to taste
Water as needed

Garlic Oil:

3–4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
½–1 cup cooking oil

Seasoned Beef:

250 g boiled beef, thinly sliced
2–3 tbsp light soy sauce
1 tbsp sweet soy sauce
1 tsp ground black pepper
2–3 tbsp garlic oil

Soy Chili Sauce:

8–10 red bird’s eye chilies
2 cloves garlic
3–4 tbsp water
3–4 tbsp sweet soy sauce
1 tsp sugar
Juice of 1 calamnansi (lime)

Noodles:

4 portions kolok noodles
3–4 tbsp garlic oil
1–2 tbsp fish sauce
1–2 tbsp sweet soy sauce
1 tbsp vinegar

Toppings:

Spring onions & celery leaves, sliced
Calamansi (lime)
Fish cake & fish balls
Mustard greens, blanched
Tomato wedges
Fried garlic or fried shallots

Preparation Steps

1
Soup: Boil the beef in water and discard the first boil. Add fresh water, soup spices, stock cube, and salt. Simmer until tender. Remove the beef and set aside, keeping the soup.
2
Garlic oil: Fry garlic in oil until golden. Set the fried garlic aside as garnish and reserve the oil for cooking.
3
Beef: Marinate the beef for 30 minutes. Stir-fry quickly over high heat with garlic oil for 2–3 minutes until just cooked. Add a little soup if needed.
4
Noodles: Blanch the noodles and toss with garlic oil, soy sauces, fish sauce, and vinegar until evenly coated.
5
Soy Chili Oil: Blend chilies and garlic, then mix with the remaining ingredients.
6
To Serve: Toss noodles with a little chili soy sauce, top with beef and garnishes, and serve with hot soup sprinkled with spring onions and celery leaves.
