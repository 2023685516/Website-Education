Rendang Tok

A dry, dark beef rendang that is slow-cooked for hours. It features a complex blend of spices, thick coconut milk, and toasted coconut (kerisik) to create its signature deep color and rich taste.

5 Hours
Heavy
Traditional

Protein & Base

2kg Beef (Solid Lean Cut)
Thick Coconut Milk (from 4 coconuts)
4 tbsp Toasted Coconut (Kerisik)
Palm Sugar (Gula Melaka)

Aromatic Paste

500g Shallots (Bawang Merah)
12 cloves Garlic
7 stalks Lemongrass (Serai)
Galangal & Ginger

Dried Spices

Coriander & Cumin Seeds
Black Pepper Seeds
Cinnamon, Star Anise & Cloves
Chili Powder / Dried Chili Paste

Step-by-step Rendang Tok Perak Tutorial by Che Nom

Preparation Steps

1
Preparation: Toast the coriander, cumin, and black pepper until fragrant. Grind them into a fine powder. Blend the fresh shallots, garlic, lemongrass, and galangal into a smooth paste.
2
The Marriage: Place the beef cubes in a large wok. Mix in the blended paste, ground dry spices, and chili powder. Coat the beef thoroughly—traditional Rendang Tok often skips the sautéing step to allow the coconut milk to release its own oil later. [00:05:31]
3
Simmering: Pour in the thick coconut milk. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Once it boils, add the toasted coconut (kerisik) and palm sugar. [00:07:42]
4
The Dark Finish: Continue slow-cooking for several hours (up to 5 hours) until the liquid has completely evaporated, the oil has separated, and the beef is tender and dark brown. Stir constantly in the final stages to prevent burning. Serve with Lemang or Ketupat. [00:09:08]
