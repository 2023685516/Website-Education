Nasi Dagang Terengganu

A beloved Terengganu classic featuring coconut-infused rice with a lightly sticky texture, perfectly paired with a rich, spicy tuna curry and refreshing acar timun.

90 Mins
Medium
6-8 Pax

Ingredients

Untuk Nasi:

4 cawan beras jati/wangi
1 cawan beras pulut
2 cawan santan pekat & 2 cawan santan cair/air
Halba, bawang merah & halia (dihiris)
Garam & gula secukup rasa

Gulai Ikan (Simple Version):

500g-800g ikan tongkol/kembung
3 sudu besar cili boh
Bawang merah, putih & halia (dikisar)
2 cawan santan & air asam jawa

Acar Timun:

Timun, lobak merah & bawang
Cuka, gula & garam

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare the Rice: Rinse the jasmine and glutinous rice until the water runs clear. Soak the rice for 4–8 hours (overnight is ideal). Drain and steam for 15–20 minutes until partially cooked.
2
Coconut Infusion: Mix thick and thin coconut milk with salt, sugar, sliced shallots, ginger, and fenugreek seeds. Pour this over the partially steamed rice, fold gently, and steam again for 20–30 minutes until fully cooked and fragrant.
3
Cook the Fish Curry: Sauté the aromatics and chili paste until oil separates. Add fish chunks, coconut milk, and tamarind juice. Simmer gently until the fish is cooked and the gravy reaches a rich thickness.
4
Prepare Acar: Slice the vegetables and toss them with a mixture of vinegar, sugar, and salt. Chill for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld and provide a bright contrast to the rich rice.
5
Serve: Scoop the coconut rice onto a plate, ladle the fish curry generously on the side, and serve with a fresh portion of acar timun and a hard-boiled egg if desired.
