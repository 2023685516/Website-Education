Kuih Keria

Kuih Keria is a sweet potato doughnut, crispy on the outside, soft inside, and coated with rich, sweet brown sugar.

45 Minutes
Easy
4-5 Servings

BAHAN DOH

1 kg ubi keledek oren
170gm tepung gandum
20gm tepung beras (cap bunga teratai)
3gm baking powder
15gm mentega
2 cubit garam

BAHAN SIRA (GLAZE)

100-150gm gula melaka ketul
30gm gula pasir
1/2 cawan air
2 helai daun pandan

How to Make Kuih Keria Gula Melaka

Learn the secret to making perfectly soft sweet potato donuts with a crunchy palm sugar glaze.

Watch Tutorial

COOKING STEPS

1
Steam or boil the sweet potatoes until tender. Drain well and set aside to cool completely.
2
Mash the sweet potatoes using a mortar pestle or masher until smooth and no lumps remain.
3
Add butter, salt, wheat flour, rice flour, and baking powder into the mashed potatoes. Knead until well combined.
4
If the dough is sticky, do not add more flour. Instead, wash and dry your hands, then apply a bit of flour or oil to your palms.
5
Weigh the dough into 35g portions. Shape into balls and create a hole in the center using a chopstick or your finger.
6
Deep fry the donuts until golden brown and crispy. Remove from oil and drain well.
7
For the glaze: Melt palm sugar, granulated sugar, pandan leaves, and water in a pan over low heat until the mixture becomes thick and bubbly.
8
Once the glaze is thick and syrupy, add the fried keria. Toss gently on very low heat until every piece is evenly coated. Turn off the heat.
9
Transfer to a tray and spread them out (do not stack them). Serve once the glaze has cooled and hardened.
