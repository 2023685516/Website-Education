Puding Diraja (Royal Pudding)

A classic Malay dessert from Pekan, Pahang. It features golden bananas, delicate "Jala Emas" (golden threads), and a rich custard sauce, garnished with prunes, cherries, and cashew nuts.

60 Mins
Hard
Pahang Heritage

Ingredients

Bahan Pisang:

10–12 biji pisang lemak manis
Minyak untuk menggoreng

Bahan Jala Emas:

5–7 biji kuning telur
1–2 cawan gula pasir
2–3 cawan air
1 helai daun pandan

Sos Kastard:

1 tin susu cair
2–3 biji kuning telur
125–200g gula
1 sudu tepung kastard

Hiasan:

Prune, Ceri & Kacang Gajus

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare the Bananas: Fry the bananas whole in hot oil until golden brown. Drain and set aside.
2
Make Jala Emas: Boil water, sugar, and pandan leaf to create a syrup. Whisk egg yolks lightly and pour them through a funnel (or jala mould) into the boiling syrup in a circular motion. Once cooked, lift and drain.
3
Cook the Custard Sauce: Whisk together evaporated milk, egg yolks, custard flour, sugar, and vanilla. Cook over low heat (or double boiler) until the sauce thickens and becomes smooth.
4
Assembly: Arrange the fried bananas in a serving dish. Top with a layer of Jala Emas, then sprinkle prunes, cherries, and toasted cashews.
5
Serve Chilled: Pour the custard sauce over the ingredients. Chill in the refrigerator before serving for the best taste.
