Penang Pasembur

Known as "Mamak Rojak," this dish is a vibrant mix of crispy fritters and fresh vegetables drenched in a sweet and spicy peanut potato gravy.

20 Mins
Snack
3-4 Servings

Fresh Base

1 Cucumber (shredded)
1 Jicama / Turnip (shredded)
2 Potatoes (boiled & sliced)
Handful of Bean Sprouts

Crispy Fritters

Prawn Fritters (Cucur Udang)
Fried Beancurd (Tofu)
Fried Flour Crackers

Sweet & Spicy Sauce

Sweet Potato (boiled & mashed)
Peanuts (roasted & crushed)
Dried Chili Paste & Sugar

Watch Recipe Tutorial

How to make the perfect Pasembur Gravy

Preparation Steps

1
The Gravy: Saut√© chili paste until oil separates. Add mashed sweet potatoes and water to create a thick base. Stir in crushed peanuts, sugar, and salt to taste. Let it simmer until glossy.
2
Prep the Shreds: Use a grater to shred the cucumber and jicama into long, thin strips. Blanch the bean sprouts briefly in hot water.
3
The Crunch: Chop the crispy prawn fritters and fried tofu into bite-sized pieces. If you have crackers, break them into smaller shards.
4
Plating: Mound the shredded vegetables in the center of a plate. Surround with fritters and tofu. Pour a generous amount of warm sweet potato gravy over everything and serve immediately.
