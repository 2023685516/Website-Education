Laksam

Laksam is a beloved East Coast Malaysian specialty — thick rice noodles rolled and served with a rich, creamy fish‑based coconut gravy, sambal chili, and fresh vegetables (ulam).

60 Mins
Medium
4-6 Servings

Ingredients

Untuk Mi Laksam:

3 cawan tepung beras
1½ sudu besar tepung gandum
2 cawan air
¼ sudu kecil garam

Untuk Kuah Laksam:

200g ikan kembung (direbus)
2–3 keping asam keping
1 batang serai (diketuk)
Bawang merah, putih & halia (kisar)
120 ml santan pekat
Gula Melaka & garam

Sambal & Ulam:

Cili merah, cili padi & belacan
Bunga kantan & kacang panjang
Timun, taugeh & daun kesum

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Make the Laksam Noodles: Mix rice flour, wheat flour, salt, and water until smooth. Pour a thin layer onto a lightly oiled baking pan and steam for 6–8 minutes. Remove, cool slightly, roll tightly, and cut into pieces.
2
Prepare the Fish: Boil mackerel with tamarind slices and lemongrass. Once cooked, strain and flake the fish meat, removing all bones.
3
Cook the Gravy: Blend the flaked fish meat with shallots, garlic, and ginger. Return to the pot, add coconut milk, tamarind slices, and palm sugar. Simmer on low heat while stirring until thickened.
4
Prepare Sambal: Grind or blend shrimp paste (belacan) with red chilies, bird's eye chilies, and lime juice. Season with a pinch of salt and sugar.
5
Assemble & Serve: Place the rolled noodles in a bowl, pour the warm fish‑coconut gravy over them. Top with a spoonful of sambal and plenty of fresh ulam (cucumber, bean sprouts, and torch ginger).
