Mee Rebus Ramli

A beloved Perak treasure. These yellow noodles are drenched in a thick, velvety sweet-and-spicy gravy crafted from boiled sweet potatoes, complemented by crunchy fritters and a perfectly balanced spice profile.

75 Mins
Moderate
Traditional

The Thick Gravy

500g Sweet Potatoes (boiled & mashed)
Dried Shrimp (finely ground)
Lemongrass & Galangal
Chili Paste

Main & Sides

500g Fresh Yellow Noodles
Blanched Bean Sprouts
Hard-boiled Eggs
Fried Tofu (Tauhu Goreng)

The Garnish

Crispy Cucur (Fritters)
Fresh Green Chilies
Fried Shallots
Calamansi Lime (Limau Kasturi)

Experience the Shop

A glimpse into the famous Mee Rebus Ramli in Ipoh

Preparation Steps

1
The Potato Base: Boil the sweet potatoes until soft. Peel and blend with water into a smooth paste. This gives the gravy its signature body and natural sweetness.
2
Building the Flavor: Heat oil and saut√© the chili paste with lemongrass and ground dried shrimp until the oil separates. Stir in the sweet potato paste and add beef or prawn stock for depth. Simmer until the gravy is thick and glossy.
3
Noodle Prep: Blanch the yellow noodles and bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 minutes. Drain well and portion into deep serving bowls.
4
The Assembly: Pour the steaming hot gravy over the noodles. Top with half an egg, fried tofu, and plenty of crispy fritters. Garnish with sliced green chilies, fried shallots, and a squeeze of calamansi lime for the perfect finish.
