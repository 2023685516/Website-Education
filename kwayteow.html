Penang Char Kway Teow

A smoky, savory stir-fried noodle dish that is synonymous with Penang. It features high-heat cooking to achieve "Wok Hei" (breath of the wok).

25 Mins
High Heat
1-2 Servings

Noodles & Base

300g Flat Rice Noodles (Kway Teow)
2 cloves Garlic (minced)
Lard or Vegetable Oil

Proteins & Veggies

6 Large Prawns
Cockles (Kerang)
1 Egg
Bean Sprouts & Chives

The Sauce Mix

Light & Dark Soy Sauce
Oyster Sauce
Chili Paste (optional)

Authentic Michelin-style Char Kway Teow Tutorial

Preparation Steps

1
High Heat Start: Heat your wok until it is smoking. Add oil/lard and saut√© minced garlic and prawns until the prawns are half-cooked.
2
Noodle Sear: Add the flat rice noodles and spread them out. Pour in your prepared sauce mix and chili paste. Stir-fry rapidly on maximum heat to get the smoky "Wok Hei."
3
The Final Mix: Push noodles to the side, crack an egg into the center, and scramble it slightly before mixing it back into the noodles.
4
The Crunch: Quickly toss in the bean sprouts, chives, and cockles. Stir for another 30 seconds until the sprouts are just wilted but still crunchy. Serve immediately!
