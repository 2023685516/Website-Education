Keropok Lekor

A classic Malaysian fish sausage made with fresh fish and sago flour. Whether boiled for a chewy texture or fried for a crispy golden finish, it is best enjoyed with spicy sweet chili sauce.

30 Mins
Medium
10-12 Pcs

Ingredients

1 kg isi ikan (kembung/selayang)
185g-500g tepung sagu (ikut tekstur)
Garam secukup rasa (1-2 sudu teh)
1 sudu teh baking powder (pilihan)
50g ketul ais (untuk uli)
Air & minyak masak

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare the Fish: Clean and debone the fish. Remove the skin and mince or blend the fillets until they form a smooth paste.
2
Make the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the minced fish with salt and baking powder. Gradually add sago flour and knead. Add ice cubes while mixing to keep the dough firm and manageable.
3
Shape the Lekor: Dust your work surface with extra sago flour. Roll portions of the dough into long cylindrical sticks (about 2cm in diameter).
4
Cook the Lekor: Bring a large pot of water to a boil and drop the sticks in. Once they float, they are cooked. For the crispy version, slice the boiled lekor and deep-fry until golden brown.
5
Serve: Serve hot with a side of spicy sweet chili sauce. Perfect as an afternoon snack or appetizer!
