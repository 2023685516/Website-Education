Kek Lapis Sarawak

Moist and flavorful layered cake from Sarawak loved for its striking colors and rich taste.

2 Hours
Hard
4-5 Servings

Ingredients

500 g salted butter, slightly softened
200 g caster sugar
1 tbsp ovalette
10 eggs
250 g sweetened condensed milk
1.5 tsp vanilla extract
220 g cake flour

Preparation Steps

1
Preparation: Cut 500 g salted butter into small cubes; keep chilled but soft.
2
Grease an 8×8 inch (3-inch high) pan and line with parchment paper; grease again.
3
Sift 220 g cake flour and set aside.
4
Crack 10 room-temperature eggs into a separate bowl.
5
Preheat oven (top heat/grill) to 190–200°C with the rack in the middle.
1
Making the Batter: Beat butter until creamy and pale. Add caster sugar and beat 6–8 minutes until light and fluffy.
2
Mix in 1 tbsp ovalette until just combined.
3
Add eggs one at a time on medium speed, ensuring each is fully incorporated. Beat 5–7 minutes until fluffy.
4
Fold in condensed milk and vanilla briefly on low speed.
5
Gradually add sifted flour (3–4 additions), mixing very lightly. Stop the mixer and gently fold with a spatula—do not overmix.
1
Layering & Baking: Portion batter (about 110 g per layer). Spread evenly in the pan.
2
Bake first layer 7–8 minutes until lightly browned. Press gently to compact.
3
Add next layer, bake 4–5 minutes, repeat until all layers are done.
4
Bake final layer 4–5 minutes, then reduce oven to 150–160°C and bake 10 more minutes for even cooking.
1
Cooling & Storage: Let cake cool completely in the pan on a rack.
2
Run a thin knife around edges, invert onto a board, and remove parchment.
3
Trim edges if needed, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight to set the texture and flavor.
