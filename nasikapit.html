Nasi Kapit Sambal Kelapa

A classic Terengganu breakfast. Pressed rice cakes (Nasi Kapit) served with a savory, sweet coconut and fish floss known as Sambal Kelapa. It's a perfect balance of smoky and salty flavors.

40 Mins
Easy
4-5 Pax

Ingredients

A) Nasi Kapit (Nasi Himpit)

4–6 cawan nasi putih
(Boleh guna nasi himpit segera)

B) Sambal Kelapa

4–6 ekor ikan selayang / kembung
1 biji kelapa parut
1–2 keping asam keping
Garam & Gula melaka secukupnya

Bahan Kisar/Tumbuk:

5–8 ulas bawang merah
2–3 ulas bawang putih
1 inci halia & 4 batang serai
1–2 sudu lada hitam (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare the Rice: Cook white rice as usual or boil instant rice packets. Press the cooked rice while warm to make it compact so it doesn't crumble when dipped in sambal.
2
Prep the Fish: Boil the fish (ikan selayang/kembung) with salt and tamarind peel (asam keping). Once cooked, remove the flesh and discard the bones.
3
Toast Coconut: Fry the grated coconut in a dry pan (without oil) until it turns golden brown and crispy.
4
Blend & Cook: Grind the aromatics (onions, ginger, lemongrass, black pepper) until fine. Mix this with the toasted coconut and fish flesh.
5
Season & Serve: Cook the mixture over low heat. Add Gula Melaka and salt. Stir until the sambal is slightly dry but still moist and flavorful. East Coast style favors a sweet-savory balance!
