Asam Pedas Ikan Pari

A classic Malaysian dish, famous for its spicy and sour profile, perfectly balanced with aromatic herbs and fresh fish.

1 Hour 25 Minutes
Sederhana
4 People

BAHAN KISAR

2 biji bawang besar (sederhana)
3 biji bawang putih
2 inci halia
2 inci kunyit hidup
Sedikit belacan
1 sudu besar lada hitam

RAMUAN UTAMA

1 mangkuk cili kering (dikisar asing)
1 sudu besar halba
1 sudu kecil gula (atau gula melaka)
2 batang serai (diketuk)
1 sudu pes Asam Jawa
1 mangkuk air
Sayur bendi & Daun kesum
4 ketul ikan pari
1 kuntum bunga kantan (belah dua)
Garam & Tomato

How to Cook Asam Pedas Melaka

Watch the secret technique to sautéing the chili paste until it separates beautifully for that authentic taste.

Watch Tutorial

COOKING STEPS

1
Heat oil in a pot and fry 1 tablespoon of fenugreek (halba) until it turns golden brown and fragrant.
2
Add the blended base ingredients and one small bowl of chili paste. Sauté until the oil separates (pecah minyak).
3
Add 1 teaspoon of sugar. You may use palm sugar (gula melaka) for a richer flavor.
4
Add 2 bruised lemongrass stalks and 1 tablespoon of tamarind paste. Stir well to combine.
5
Pour in 1 bowl of water. Stir and let it come to a simmer.
6
Add the okra (bendi) and Vietnamese coriander (daun kesum). Boil until the okra is partially tender.
7
Now, add the fish. Stingray is used in this recipe, but you can use any fish of your choice.
8
Add the halved torch ginger flower (bunga kantan) and cook until its aromatic scent is released.
9
Season with salt to taste. Give it one last gentle stir.
10
Finally, add the fresh tomatoes and turn off the heat. Ready to serve!
