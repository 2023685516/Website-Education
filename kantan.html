Sambal Bunga Kantan

A unique Malaysian condiment featuring the highly aromatic Torch Ginger flower. It offers a refreshing, citrusy, and floral profile that perfectly complements spicy chilies and crispy anchovies.

40 Mins
Easy
3-4 Pax

Ingredients

3–4 kuntum bunga kantan (hiris nipis)
3–5 biji cili padi (ikut pedas)
1 biji bawang besar (hiris)
3–5 ulas bawang putih (hiris)
Ikan bilis goreng garing (pilihan)
Minyak masak
Garam & gula secukup rasa

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the sliced onions, garlic, and bird's eye chilies. Sauté until the mixture is fragrant and the onions turn translucent.
2
Add Torch Ginger: Toss in the finely sliced bunga kantan (torch ginger). Stir constantly with the sautéed ingredients until the flower softens and releases its signature citrusy aroma.
3
Season: Add salt and sugar to taste. If the sambal feels too dry, you can splash in a little water to help the flavors combine and soften the texture.
4
Add Crispy Elements: If you are using fried anchovies, add them at the very last minute. Give it a quick toss to ensure they are coated in the sambal while maintaining their crunch.
5
Finish and Serve: Cook until the sambal reaches your desired consistency. Remove from heat and allow it to cool slightly before serving as a side with steamed rice or grilled fish.
