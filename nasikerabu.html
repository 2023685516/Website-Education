Nasi Kerabu

An iconic dish featuring blue butterfly pea flower rice, served with stuffed chilies (solok lada) and fresh aromatic herbs that stimulate the appetite.

2 Hours
Medium
6 Servings

Ulam-ulaman

Daun samok & Daun mata ayam
Timun batang & Taugeh
Kacang panjang & Bunga kantan
Daun kesum

Bahan Lauk:

Ikan mentah (Kembung/Selayang)
Lada besar (untuk Solok)
Kelapa larut & Santan pekat
Budu & Gula Melaka (Nisan)

Step-by-step

1
Blue Rice (Nasi Biru): Extract the natural pigment from butterfly pea flowers by soaking or squeezing them in water. Cook the rice as usual using this blue extract, and add kaffir lime leaves to infuse the rice with a refreshing aroma.
2
Solok Lada: Deseed the large chilies and stuff them with a mixture of pounded fish meat, grated coconut, black pepper, shallots, ginger, and egg. Simmer gently in thick coconut milk over low heat until the stuffing is firm and cooked through.
3
Kuah Tumis: Saut√© the blended ingredients (dried chilies, ginger, shrimp paste, and lemongrass) until fragrant and crisp. Pour in the coconut milk, add bruised lemongrass, budu (fermented fish sauce), and palm sugar. Simmer until the oil rises to the surface (pecah minyak).
4
Fish Coconut Garnish(Sambal Kelapa Ikan): Lightly toast the grated coconut until golden. Combine it with boiled fish meat and a blended paste of onions, lemongrass, and ginger. Fry the mixture until it reaches a dry, crispy texture, then season to taste.
5
Budu & Chili Sauce(Air Lada): For the budu sauce, simmer fermented fish sauce with water, palm sugar, lemongrass, and kaffir lime leaves. For the chili sauce (air lada), blend fresh chilies with pickled garlic, vinegar, salt, and sugar until well combined.
6
Grilled Proteins: Marinate your choice of chicken or beef with a blend of aromatic spices. Add an extra touch of palm sugar to the marinade to ensure the meat gets a beautiful, glossy glaze once grilled to perfection.
